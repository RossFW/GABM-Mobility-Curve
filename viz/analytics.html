Author Note — OLS Scatter
OLS Summary: Baseline Caution vs. Behavioral Sensitivity
Each point is one of the 21 LLM configurations. X-axis: β₀ (intercept) — predicted mobility at 0% new cases, measuring default activity level. Y-axis: β₁ (linear coefficient) — linear term from quadratic OLS (Y = β₀ + β₁X + β₂X²), measuring behavioral sensitivity. Hover a point to identify the model.
Author Note — Sensitivity Ranking
Behavioral Sensitivity Ranking (β slope)
All 21 configurations ranked by β₁ (linear coefficient from quadratic OLS). More negative values indicate a stronger reduction in mobility per unit of new cases (stronger behavioral response).

Does training data recency predict how strongly a model responds to rising infection? X-axis: knowledge cutoff (year). Y-axis: β slope from OLS. Line shows OLS fit across all 21 configurations.
